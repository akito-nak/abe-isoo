## Study in America

Abe Isoo traveled to the United States in 1890 to study at Hartford Seminary in Connecticut. He remained there from 1890 to 1895, receiving theological education.

## Hartford Seminary

Hartford Seminary was a Protestant theological institution founded in 1834. The seminary provided theological education and was influenced by the Social Gospel movement during Abe's time there.

## Social Gospel Movement

The Social Gospel movement emphasized the application of Christian principles to social problems such as poverty and inequality. This theological approach influenced Protestant seminaries during the late 19th century.

## Return to Japan

Abe Isoo returned to Japan in 1895 after completing his studies. He began teaching at Waseda University in 1899.

## Later Career

After returning to Japan, Abe Isoo became involved in education and social reform activities. He was one of the founding members of Japan's first socialist political party in 1901. He also played a role in establishing baseball at Waseda University.
